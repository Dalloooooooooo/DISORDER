In a reality where mental health is often treated as an accessory, and where an estimated 4,4% of the global population lives with an Anxiety Disorder (WHO), research on social stigma shows that anxiety disorder is frequently not believed, dismissed as a lie, victimhood, or a mere whim. Anxiety Disorder often remains invisible from the outside, because it tends to exist entirely within the inner space of the individual, hidden, unseen.

This project originates from my Master’s Thesis in Communication Design at Politecnico di Milano and aims to investigate how audiovisual language, and video art in particular, can make perceptible and visible the inner dimension of people living with Anxiety Disorder: a reality that is valid, vast, and profoundly real.

This library specifically is a curated archive of audiovisual elements, systematically categorized and specifically developed for use in five interviews with individuals experiencing Anxiety Disorder, to facilitate the audiovisual translation of their anxiety attack experiences through
a guided process of selection among different elements.

All elements within the library are copyright free, licensed under Creative Commons, or consist of edited materials derived from copyright free assets, ensuring their accessibility and usability for research purposes.
